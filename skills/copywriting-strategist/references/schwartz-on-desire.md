# Schwartz on Desire

This document contains key insights from Eugene Schwartz on channeling mass desire, taken from his book *Breakthrough Advertising*.

## The Core Principle

> "Copy cannot create desire for a product. It can only take the hopes, dreams, fears and desires that already exist in the hearts of millions of people, and focus those already-existing desires onto a particular product."

**Key Insight:** The copywriter's job is NOT to create desire, but to channel and direct existing desire. You cannot create mass desire through advertising—you can only exploit it.

## The Five States of Awareness

1.  **Most Aware:** The customer knows your product, and only needs to know "the deal."
2.  **Product-Aware:** The customer knows what you sell, but isn't convinced it's for them.
3.  **Solution-Aware:** The customer knows the result they want, but not that your product provides it.
4.  **Problem-Aware:** The customer senses they have a problem, but doesn't know there's a solution.
5.  **Completely Unaware:** No problem, no desire.

## The Copywriter's Task at Each Stage

- **Most Aware:** Your headline is the deal.
- **Product-Aware:** Your headline is the product, its superiority, and the benefit.
- **Solution-Aware:** Your headline is the solution, and you prove your product delivers it.
- **Problem-Aware:** Your headline is the problem, and you crystallize it into a single, powerful image.
- **Unaware:** Your headline is the audience, and you call them out directly.
