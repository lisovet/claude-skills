# Technique 7: Camouflage

**Source:** *Breakthrough Advertising* by Eugene M. Schwartz, Chapter 13

## Core Principle

Camouflage is the art of borrowing conviction for your copy. It's about making your ad look and feel like something other than an ad—like a news article, a scientific report, or a personal letter.

## How to Borrow Believability

1.  **The News Angle:** Frame your product as a newsworthy event or a recent discovery.

2.  **The Scientific Report:** Use the language and format of a scientific paper to present your claims.

3.  **The Personal Letter:** Write your ad as a one-to-one letter from the founder or a satisfied customer.

4.  **The Editorial Approach:** Make your ad look and read like an article from the publication it's in.

## The Power of Camouflage

By disguising your ad as a more credible form of content, you can lower the reader's natural skepticism and make them more receptive to your message.
