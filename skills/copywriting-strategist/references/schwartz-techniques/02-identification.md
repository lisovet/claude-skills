# Technique 2: Identification

**Source:** *Breakthrough Advertising* by Eugene M. Schwartz, Chapter 8

## Core Principle

Identification is the process of building a saleable personality into your product. It's about making the product more than just a collection of features—it's about giving it a character, a role, and a story that the prospect can identify with.

## The Three Types of Roles

1.  **Character Roles:** Giving the product a personality (e.g., "The Friendly Cleanser," "The Tough-as-Nails Truck").
2.  **Achievement Roles:** Positioning the product as a tool for achieving a desired status or identity (e.g., "The Executive's Pen," "The Athlete's Watch").
3.  **Physical Roles:** Emphasizing the physical look and feel of the product, making it a desirable object in itself.

## How to Build a Saleable Personality

- **The Personal Note:** Speak directly to the prospect in a personal, one-to-one tone.
- **The Roles Your Prospect Desires:** Understand the identities and aspirations of your target audience and position the product as a way to achieve them.
- **How to Put These Longings to Work:** Weave the desired roles and identities into the copy, showing how the product helps the prospect become the person they want to be.
