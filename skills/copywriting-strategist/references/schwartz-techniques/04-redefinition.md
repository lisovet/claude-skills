# Technique 4: Redefinition

**Source:** *Breakthrough Advertising* by Eugene M. Schwartz, Chapter 10

## Core Principle

Redefinition is the process of removing objections to your product by changing the way the prospect thinks about it. It's about reframing the conversation to make objections irrelevant.

## How to Remove Objections

1.  **Simplification:** Take a complex product or idea and make it simple and easy to understand.

2.  **Escalation:** Take a small benefit and show how it leads to a much larger, more desirable outcome.

3.  **Price Reduction:** Reframe the price to make it seem smaller or more manageable (e.g., "less than a cup of coffee a day").

## The Power of Reframing

By redefining the product, the problem, or the price, you can sidestep objections before they even arise. For example:

-   Instead of selling a "diet plan," you sell a "lifestyle change."
-   Instead of selling an "expensive camera," you sell a "tool for capturing priceless memories."
-   Instead of selling a "complex software," you sell a "simple way to get more done."
