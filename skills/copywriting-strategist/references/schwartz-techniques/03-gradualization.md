# Technique 3: Gradualization

**Source:** *Breakthrough Advertising* by Eugene M. Schwartz, Chapter 9

## Core Principle

Gradualization is the art of making your prospect believe your claims before you even state them. It's about building a foundation of belief, brick by brick, so that when you make your big promise, the prospect is already nodding in agreement.

## The Architecture of Belief

Schwartz outlines a process for building belief gradually:

1.  **Start with a Statement the Prospect Already Believes:** Begin with a fact or a premise that is universally accepted or easily verifiable.

2.  **Build a Chain of Logic:** Link your initial statement to a series of subsequent statements, each one logically following from the last.

3.  **Introduce Your Product as the Inevitable Conclusion:** By the time you introduce your product, it should feel like the natural and only solution to the problem you've laid out.

4.  **Use Syllogistic Thinking:** Structure your argument as a logical syllogism (e.g., "All A is B. C is A. Therefore, C is B.").

## Key Tactics

- **Contradiction of Present (False) Beliefs:** Gently challenge the prospect's existing beliefs by presenting new information or a different perspective.
- **The Language of Logic:** Use words and phrases that signal a logical argument (e.g., "therefore," "because," "it follows that").
- **Detailed Identification:** Provide specific details and evidence to support each step in your logical chain.
